# Self-Managed Strata - Feature Gap Analysis

Analysis of gaps from the perspective of individual, self-managed stratas run by volunteer council members.

---

## Governance & Compliance

### 1. No Voting / Resolution System

This is arguably more critical for self-managed stratas than for managed ones. Volunteer councils need to run AGMs/SGMs with proper quorum, special resolution thresholds, and proxy tracking — and they don't have a professional guiding them through it. Online/async voting would be especially valuable since getting quorum at in-person meetings is a perennial struggle.

### 2. No Bylaw Registry

Self-managed councils frequently don't even know what their own bylaws say. A structured bylaw repository with version history, plain-language summaries, and the ability to track amendments through resolutions would save hours of digging through old minutes.

### 3. No Compliance Calendar

Volunteer councils miss deadlines because nobody's job is to track them. AGM deadlines, insurance renewals, depreciation report schedules, fire safety inspections — a compliance calendar with automated reminders would prevent the most common self-managed strata failures.

---

## Financial

### 4. No Unit Registry with Entitlements

Same gap as for management companies, but the driver is different. Self-managed councils need this to calculate levies fairly and to know who actually owns what (especially after sales). Currently there's no unit concept at all.

### 5. No Levy / Strata Fee Management

The single biggest financial task for any strata. Councils need to set annual budgets, calculate per-unit levies based on entitlements, generate recurring invoices, and track who's in arrears. The current invoice system requires creating each one manually — unworkable for a 50-unit building doing monthly levies.

### 6. No Operating vs. Contingency Reserve Fund Tracking

Most strata legislation requires maintaining a contingency reserve fund separate from operating. Councils need visibility into fund balances, contribution schedules, and whether they're meeting minimum funding requirements.

### 7. No Special Levy Support

When a big expense hits (roof replacement, elevator upgrade), councils raise special levies. This requires resolution tracking, per-unit calculation, payment plan support, and separate accounting from regular levies.

---

## Owner Experience

### 8. No Owner Self-Service for Common Requests

Owners regularly need to: update their contact info, submit renovation/alteration requests, register tenants or pets, request move-in/move-out bookings, and get parking passes. None of these workflows exist — everything goes through the inbox as unstructured messages.

### 9. No Announcement / Notice Board

The inbox blast exists but it's email-based. There's no persistent notice board or announcement feed on the dashboard where owners can see current notices (water shutoff tomorrow, elevator maintenance next week, AGM notice).

### 10. No Document Access for Owners

The files system exists with public/private visibility, but there's no organized document library where owners can self-serve common documents (bylaws, rules, insurance certificates, meeting minutes, financial statements). The flat file list doesn't provide the categorization owners expect.

---

## Council Operations

### 11. No Council Handoff / Onboarding

When council members change at an AGM (which happens constantly in self-managed stratas), there's no structured handoff. No checklist, no knowledge base, no way to transfer institutional knowledge. New volunteers start from scratch.

### 12. No Task Assignment Between Council Members

Volunteer councils split work informally. There's no way to assign and track tasks ("John is getting quotes for the roof, Sarah is following up with the insurance broker"). The inbox doesn't serve this purpose — it's communication, not task management.

### 13. No Maintenance Log / Building History

Self-managed stratas lose institutional knowledge when council members rotate. A maintenance log tracking what was done, when, by whom, and at what cost would build a permanent building history that survives council turnover.

---

## Communication

### 14. No Tenant Communication Channel

Many units are rented out. Tenants need to receive certain notices (fire drills, water shutoffs, move-in rules) but shouldn't see financial information. There's no tenant role or tenant-appropriate communication channel.

### 15. No Emergency Broadcast

Different from inbox blast — a high-priority push notification for emergencies (flood, fire alarm, security incident). Email-based blasts aren't urgent enough.

---

## Practical Building Management

### 16. No Key / Access Management

Who has keys to the mechanical room? What's the gate code? Where are the water shutoff valves? Self-managed councils need a secure reference for building access and emergency procedures.

### 17. No Contractor / Vendor Contacts

Less structured than a management company needs, but councils still need a shared contact list for the plumber, electrician, elevator company, landscaper, etc. — with contract details and renewal dates.

### 18. No Insurance Certificate Distribution

Owners and their mortgage lenders regularly request proof of strata insurance. There's no streamlined way to share current certificates or track requests.

---

## Priority Summary

| Priority | Feature | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **P0** | Unit registry with entitlements | Foundation for levies, voting, ownership tracking |
| **P0** | Levy/strata fee management + bulk invoicing | Core financial workflow, currently unworkable at scale |
| **P0** | Voting / resolutions | Can't run AGMs properly without it |
| **P0** | Compliance calendar with reminders | Volunteers miss deadlines without automation |
| **P1** | Owner self-service requests (renovations, move-ins, etc.) | Reduces council burden, improves owner experience |
| **P1** | Announcement / notice board | Persistent visibility, not just email |
| **P1** | Operating vs. reserve fund tracking | Legally required separation in most jurisdictions |
| **P1** | Organized document library | Owners need self-serve access to key documents |
| **P2** | Task assignment for council members | Volunteer coordination |
| **P2** | Maintenance log / building history | Institutional knowledge retention |
| **P2** | Tenant role + communication | Common in mixed owner/rental buildings |

---

The overlap with the management company analysis is mainly in the foundational pieces (unit registry, levies, voting). But the self-managed perspective cares more about **reducing volunteer burden** (compliance reminders, self-service, templates) and **surviving council turnover** (maintenance logs, handoff tools, document organization) than about portfolio-scale efficiency.
